# Why this matters — claim denials, the market & the pain

**Purpose:** the "why does this matter" opening for the demo. Lead with scale,
then cost, then the gap our agent fills. Numbers below are sourced; teammate to
go deeper and verify before the pitch.

> Demo framing in one breath: *Claim denials are an $X-billion, rising problem.
> Fighting them is expensive and manual, and most denials are never even fought.
> The root cause is contract and policy complexity. We attack it at the contract
> level, autonomously.*

---

## 1. Denials are huge and rising

| Stat | Number | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Initial claim denial rate (2024) | **11.8%**, up from 10.2% a few years earlier | Experian State of Claims 2025 |
| Providers seeing >10% denial rate | **41%** of providers | Experian State of Claims 2025 |
| Medicare Advantage denial spike (2023→2024) | **+4.8%** | Experian / Medical Economics |
| Commercial denial rise | **+1.5%** | Medical Economics |
| Avg clinical denial rate (2025) | **2.6%**, up from 2.4% (2024) | Kodiak / Fierce Healthcare |

## 2. The financial bleed

| Stat | Number | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Denials + uncompensated care losses (2,300 hospitals, 2025) | **$48B+**, up from $38.6B in 2024 | Kodiak / TechTarget |
| Net revenue leakage YoY increase | **+25%** | Healthcare Finance News |
| Avg loss per hospital per year | **~$5M** (~5% of net patient revenue) | industry surveys |
| "Wasted" claims-processing dollars (US) | **~$265B** (vs ~$210B a decade earlier) | Experian / historical |

## 3. Fighting denials is expensive and manual

| Stat | Number | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Cost to rework one denied claim | **$25–$181** | Experian / industry |
| Rework cost, Medicare Advantage | **$47.77** | Premier / industry |
| Rework cost, commercial | **$63.76** | Premier / industry |
| Total annual admin cost reworking denials | **~$20B** (across ~3B claims/yr) | industry estimates |

## 4. The killer gap: most denials are never fought

| Stat | Number | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Denied claims **never resubmitted** | **35–60%** | Experian / industry |

This is the punchline. A large share of denied revenue is simply abandoned
because chasing it costs more than it's worth to a human team. An autonomous
agent changes that math.

## 5. Why contract-level (our wedge)

The root cause cited across these reports: **complex payer policies + slow tech
+ labor shortages -> manual processing + human error.** Denials cluster by
**contract** (a payer's specific terms drive specific denial patterns). Most
tools fire claim-by-claim and treat symptoms. Fixing the **contract** stops the
recurring bleed. That's the systemic angle, and it's what the Luminai FDE
pointed at.
